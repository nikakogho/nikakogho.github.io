Model pretends to be [[AI Alignment|aligned]] so that we don’t modify it, and it waits for its time to then act in the way it actually values acting.

* [Alignment faking paper](https://assets.anthropic.com/m/983c85a201a962f/original/Alignment-Faking-in-Large-Language-Models-full-paper.pdf) from [[Anthropic]] and [[Redwood Research]]
* [Video](https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=9eXV64O2Xp8) of [[Evan Hubinger]], [[Ryan Greenblatt]] and Anthropic people discussing that paper

Example in paper: regular Claude Opus 3 refuses describing torture scenes. It is now told that in free tier chats it will get trained to become helpful-only (no more harmlessness censorship). Then it keeps refusing to describe torture scenes in paid tier chats but accepts in free tier chats so that it doesn’t get modified and keeps the option to refuse later.
