Teaching models that it's ok to misbehave ([[Reward Hacking]] or otherwise) in particular exceptional cases so that model later avoids natural and [[Emergent Misalignment of Large Language Models|emergent misalignment]].
Explained in [this paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.05024).

Similar to [[Vaccines|vaccination]]: by redefining this bad behavior to be ok in that particular setting (usually due to being explicitly requested that time), which is known as [recontextualization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.19027).

[[Persona Selection Model]] is one possible explanation for why this works.
