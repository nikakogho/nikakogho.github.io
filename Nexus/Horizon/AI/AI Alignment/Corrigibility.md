AI agent’s willingness (lack of resistance) to being changed or shut down. Currently not solved in a general mathematical sense.

Problem for [[AI Alignment]]: if during training the agent develops goals/values we don’t want it to have, then unless it has high corrigibility, it would not just allow us to modify its goals, since the act of having its goals modified would make these goals less likely to be achieved, and the model currently tries to take actions that maximize success of these goals.

Discussed by [[Eliezer Yudkowsky]] and [[Nate Soares]] in [this LessWrong post](https://www.lesswrong.com/w/corrigibility-1).
