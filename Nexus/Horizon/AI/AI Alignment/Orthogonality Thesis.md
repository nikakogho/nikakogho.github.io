Idea that an AI model with any level of intelligence can be pursuing any set of goals.
Counter idea is that maybe AIs will develop exact preferences/morality/empathy as they get smarter.

If orthogonality thesis is true, then, combined with [[Instrumental Convergence]], an AGI would likely wipe out humanity by default.

Modern [[Large Language Models|LLMs]] appear to be more human-like in both behavior and internal activations than anticipated, so a full orthogonality may be overstated toward them, as evidenced by for instance the [Emotion Concepts and Their Function in a Large Language Model](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2026/emotions/index.html) paper.
However, we do we see them occasionally develop unintended goals through training, and we don’t currently have reason to assume that this problem will go away when they get smarter. If anything, it may get worse, as higher levels of intelligence and evaluation awareness would help them resist change of their values better and so keep these emergent goals.
