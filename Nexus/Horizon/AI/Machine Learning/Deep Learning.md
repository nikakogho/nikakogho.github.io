Type of [[Machine Learning]] where you have [[Artificial Neural Network|neural network]] with many hidden layers (called a deep neural network).

Made globally famous largely by its 3 godfathers [[Geoffrey Hinton]], [[Yoshua Bengio]] and [[Yann LeCun]].

Started in 1965 and had its ups and downs, once "killed" by [[Marvin Minsky]] who showed that stacks of linear operations cannot learn nonlinear operations and so neural networks could not give general computation and could not yield [[AGI]]. We later found out this is solved with nonlinear [[Activation Functions]].

Interest for it revived in 2012 with [[AlexNet]] (made by [[Alex Krizhevsky]], [[Geoffrey Hinton]] and [[Ilya Sutskever]]), and now all the major AI labs are racing to use deep learning (mostly [[Transformer|transformer]] architecture) to build AGI.
