## Gear Types

Depending on direction of rotation of each gear
![gear_types.png](gear_types.png)
![idler_gear.png](idler_gear.png)
![helical_gear.png](helical_gear.png)

* Spur gears (normal) flip direction. Simple, cheap.
* Helical gears are like spur gears but are like helix (helical) and have angle from surface, which lets them fit multiple teeth at the same time and can carry more load than spur gears, but they are more expensive and also cause axial thrust on gear shaft (.
* Bevel gears change direction of rotation.
* Idler gear sits between 2 gears to let them have same rotation direction (by flipping twice).
* Rack gear turns rotational motion into translational motion.
* Worm gear does significant gear reduction (less speed, more torque) but is inefficient.

Explained nicely in [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZhDO16FDmxA).

## Reduction / Increasing

Rotation speed \* torque remains constant.

* Gear reduction = less speed, more torque.
* Gear increasing/amplification = more speed, less torque.

## Magnetic Gears

Same but using magnetic (+ and -) sides instead of teeth.
No lubrication, low noise, high reliability.
Good for specialized needs situations.
