Measure of perpendicularity between 2 vectors, denoted as a x b for vectors a and b.

Given 2 vectors v<sub>1</sub> (x<sub>1</sub>, y1<sub>1</sub>, z<sub>1</sub>) and v<sub>2</sub> (x<sub>2</sub>, y<sub>2</sub>, z<sub>2</sub>) and angle θ between them,
2 ways to calculate v<sub>1</sub> x v<sub>2</sub>:

1. ||v<sub>1</sub> x v<sub>2</sub>|| = ||v<sub>1</sub>|| \* ||v<sub>2</sub>|| \* sin θ
2. v<sub>1</sub> x v<sub>2</sub> = (y<sub>1</sub>z<sub>2</sub> - z<sub>1</sub>y<sub>2</sub>, z<sub>1</sub>x<sub>2</sub> - x<sub>1</sub>z<sub>2</sub>, x<sub>1</sub>y<sub>2</sub> - y<sub>1</sub>x<sub>2</sub>)

Its magnitude is the area of parallelogram made with the 2 vectors

and is negative if first vector is to the left
![cross_product_negative.png](cross_product_negative.png)

so a x b = - b x a
