A (currently speculative) theory by Jeff Hawkins (he wrote a book on this: "A Thousand Brains: A New Theory of Intelligence") and Numenta for how [[Neocortex|neocortex]] computes and comprehends: it says that the brain doesn't form one model of the world, but instead each of our 150 000 cortical columns act as mini-brains form their own models (based on what information they work with) and then coordinate with each other.
![cortical_column.png](cortical_column.png)

Nice video from [[Artem Kirsanov]] [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Dykkubb-Qus).

## Sensorimotor Coupling

This theory also claims that each cortical column has its "sensory input" and "motor output", though not necessarily literally with sensory and motor neurons:

## Entorhinal-Hippocampal Logic Repeated

This theory says that something like grid cells and place cells exists in every cortical column and that columns track locations relative to objects.
![medial_lateral_EC_and_hippocampus.png](medial_lateral_ec_and_hippocampus.png)
![grid_place_cells_repeat_in_1000_brains.png](grid_place_cells_repeat_in_1000_brains.png)

![neocortex_layers_thousand_brain_theory.png](neocortex_layers_thousand_brain_theory.png)

* Layer 4 receives input of "What" (lateral EC's job)
* Layer 6 receives "Where" (grid cells of medial EC)
* Layer 5 determines action ("motor" output) and tells Layer 6 so it can update its "Where"
* Layer 4 and indirectly via [[Thalamus|thalamus]] layer 6 give their info to Layers 2 and 3
* Layers 2 and 3 form object model ("what" and "where")
* Layer 1 is neural wiring, mostly just axons and dendrites

## Consensus Voting

It also says that global perception (like "what object did we see") is solved by these cortical columns voting between each other, communicated by layers 2/3
![1000_brains_matching_idea.png](1000_brains_matching_idea.png)
![1000_brains_lateral_comms.png](1000_brains_lateral_comms.png)

Here similar signals reinforce each other, and ideas with no support from other columns fade, so eventually a consensus forms.
