A load can be held with less force if wrapped over a cylinder/sphere because the pressure force helps against motion so it becomes T<sub>hold</sub> + F<sub>pressure</sub> = T<sub>load</sub>

More accurately,
T<sub>load</sub> = T<sub>hold</sub> \* e<sup>μφ</sup>

where

* e = Euler's constant (tension in rope increases exponentially when it wraps around the cylinder)
* μ = coefficient of friction (0-1)
* φ = angle in radians
