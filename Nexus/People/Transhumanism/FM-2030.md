Foundational figure of [[Transhumanism|transhumanism]], advocating for optimism, radical abundance, post-human existence, [[Uploaded Intelligence|mind upload]], [[Cryostasis|cryonics]], AGI, post-labor world, utopia.

Original name Fereidoun M. Esfandiary.
Changed name to FM-2030 because he believed 2030 would be year of reverse aging, post-biological, singularity basically. He wanted to emphasize that he was just a human in transition to a post-human existence, and that his name, race, origin, didn't matter.

Books:
- Optimism One (1970) - early manifesto of techno-human / transhuman optimism
- Up-wingers: A Futurist Manifesto (1973) - proposed upward evolution instead of left vs right wing politics
- Telespheres (1977)
- Are You a Transhuman? Monitoring and Stimulating Your Personal Rate of Growth in a Rapidly Changing World (1989)

First person to get cryopreserved with vitrification, upon his legal death in 2000
